Our Mission

Hope For Hearing is a charity that gives hope and support for those suffering hearing loss and deafness in Canada. Hearing is a gift that is so important in our everyday lives that we want to help provide this gift to those who are losing it.

Why Support Our Cause?

Hearing loss is one of the leading symptoms in the word. 60% of adults in Canada have experienced some level of hearing loss.

Hearing loss severely affects everyday communication and conversations with loved ones, leading to other dangerous conditions such as dementia, Alzheimer's disease, depression, and anxiety.

More recently, hearing loss rates have also risen in children, affecting their ability to communicate with parents and learn.

More On Hearing Loss

How many people experience Hearing Loss?

Currently, 60% of adults in Canada experience hearing loss. More notably, 78% of Canada's seniors have measurable hearing loss.

How Hearing Loss affects us.

Hearing loss can make it difficult to communicate with others, which can lead to social isolation, misunderstandings, and frustration. This impacts daily life, and harms personal relationships. It can cause anxiety and depression because of this social isolation and lead to dementia and Alzheimer's.

Hearing loss often causes seniors to withdraw from social events and spend more time at home. This can lead to depression resulting in worsening eating, sleeping and physical activity habits

Is there a cure for Hearing Loss?

90% of people with hearing loss can improve their hearing, communication and quality of life through environmental changes, hearing aids, etc...

What causes Hearing Loss?

A prevalent issue that leads to a decline in hearing ability is caused by exposure to loud noises, the natural aging process, illnesses, and genetic differences. As people grow older, the likelihood of experiencing hearing loss increases, with approximately one-third of older individuals affected.
